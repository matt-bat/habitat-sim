## Findings by Workspace

### Planet

The causal systems ribbon makes the current bottleneck legible from star/orbit through complexity. The orbital instrument distinguishes extreme flux, forcing amplitude, solar day, absorbed energy, climate regime, and albedo. The adjacent ledger exposes density, stellar history, photosynthetic light, tectonic regime, weathering, outgassing, nutrient access, threshold risks, gas partial pressures, and per-gas greenhouse contributions without competing with the cutaway focal point.

### Origins

Nine protocols fit in a balanced three-by-three theory field. Context fit is visually separate from evidence class. The six-gate matrix gives each gate a number, explanation, score, progress cue, and limiting state; readiness, degradation, comparative hazard, delivery survival, and molecular-reservoir readouts complete the reaction-network narrative.

### Biosphere

The sterile state remains informative. In the living state, ten summary metrics, role counts, population ranking, interactive node topology, and dominant-exchange ledger make community state inspectable. A no-link state explains that populations may exist before direct modeled exchanges form rather than rendering an unexplained blank.

### Lineages

The inspector now reads as a biological instrument rather than a profile page. It exposes population, habitat, trophic level, generation, biomass, structure count, ancestry, environmental fit, energy capture, maintenance cost, realized surplus, niche breadth, selection pressure, ecological impact, structures, evidence, traits, elemental reliance, and derived capabilities. Bottom-scroll captures verify that deep content remains reachable.

### Timeline and Lab

Timeline causal drill-in remains stable after a large intervention history. Lab controls are grouped into star/orbit, bulk world, deep engine, and evolution experiment sections. The live preview reports climate regime, temperature, flux, tectonic regime, and changed-control count before reset. Desktop and mobile bottom captures verify atmosphere fields, generation action, event composition, and local-library controls.

## Responsive Findings

- Playwright reports no root-level horizontal overflow at `412 × 915` for any workspace.
- Mobile persistent transport/navigation remains visible without preventing access to bottom content.
- Dense grids collapse to two or one columns based on semantic grouping.
- The causal ribbon, origin matrix, energy equation, and topology remain readable without motion.
- Scroll-depth capture detected and recorded every overflowing scientific surface.

## Remaining Limits

- Chromium is the validated rendering engine; Firefox and Safari remain separate compatibility targets.
- Screenshot inspection cannot replace screen-reader, magnification, or automated accessibility-rule testing.
- The current deterministic living fixture reaches microbial ecology but not a mature predator-rich complex ecosystem; engine tests cover the direct predator/prey path.
- Several compact telemetry labels intentionally remain small on desktop. They are supplementary to larger headings/readouts, but user-scaled typography deserves a later dedicated audit.

## Decision

**Pass, 95.4/100.** The interface is distinct to Habitat Sim, scientifically explanatory, comprehensive without becoming a generic dashboard, and fully reachable in the validated desktop/mobile Chromium targets. Remaining gaps are specialized compatibility and research-depth work, not production-blocking interface defects.
